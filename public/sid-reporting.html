Detailed specifications

Daily Brief
Mon–Fri · 7:30am · ~150 words
Content — in order
  • Headline verdict — one sentence. "Quiet overnight — 2 jobs completed, no urgent flags." If there is a flag, it leads.
  • Jobs completed yesterday — count, plus any where report or invoice hasn't been raised.
  • New intakes received — count, plus any marked urgent.
  • Urgent flags — overdue intakes (past SLA), high-priority jobs with no engineer assigned.
  • Pipeline moves — deals that changed stage overnight. Brief: "2 deals moved to Quoted."
  • Invoices ready — count sitting in "ready" status awaiting send.
Tone
  • Declarative and brief. No hedging. If nothing to flag, say so directly — "Nothing urgent today."
  • Bullets only — no narrative paragraphs in the daily. Scanned, not read.
  • Numbers in bold; comparisons only when there's yesterday's figure to compare against.

Weekly Review
Monday · 7:30am · ~500 words · 3 sections
Section 1 — Needs Action
  • Jobs scheduled this week with no engineer assigned yet
  • Intakes approaching or past their SLA threshold
  • Estimates in draft for more than 7 days (stalled)
  • Deals stuck in the same stage for 14+ days
  • Invoices in "ready" status unsent for more than 3 days
Section 2 — What Moved
  • Jobs completed last week — count and job types
  • Intakes promoted to deals this week
  • Deals won or lost, with deal value
  • New customers added
  • Revenue invoiced last week (vs prior week once available)
Section 3 — Watch List
  • Engineer workload distribution — jobs per person this week
  • Revisits logged this week — early quality signal
  • High-value deals with no recent activity
  • Missing P&L upload flag if no Xero upload in 30+ days
Tone
  • Section 1 is actionable — each item should lead to a specific task in CindersX.
  • Section 2 is informational — what the team produced last week.
  • Section 3 is a watch prompt — worth attention, not panic.

Monthly Deep Dive
1st of month · 8:00am · ~1,000 words · 5 sections + meeting agenda
Section 1 — Revenue Snapshot
  • Total invoiced this month vs prior month (when available)
  • Invoice count, average value, revenue by job type (top 3)
  • P&L snapshot if a Xero upload exists for the period
  • Invoices outstanding at month end
Section 2 — Pipeline Health
  • Total pipeline value broken down by stage
  • Deals won and lost this month — conversion rate
  • Intake funnel: new → promoted → converted to job
  • Average days from intake to deal, deal to job
  • Top 3 customers by job count and deal value
Section 3 — Team Performance
  • Jobs per engineer this month
  • User activity scores (from the compute-user-scores engine)
  • Revisit rate by job type — quality indicator
  • Average days from job completion to invoice sent
Section 4 — What SID Is Learning
  • SID query volume and most-asked questions this month
  • Refusal rate — where SID doesn't yet have answers
  • Gaps SID has identified in the data it can see
  • An explicit ask: what would Zach or Jacob like SID to cover next?
Section 5 — Meeting Agenda
  • 5 suggested talking points auto-generated from the month's data
  • Each item references the specific number that surfaced it
  • One "SID doesn't know yet" item — a question the data can't answer

Design principles

🌱
Graceful when data is missing
SID never fails silently or shows broken zeros. When a prior period doesn't exist — first monthly, first week — it surfaces the gap as a positive: this is your baseline.
"No prior month to compare against yet — July is your baseline. August will show the movement."
📡
Transparent about what it knows
Every email has a brief note about where SID's picture is still incomplete — missing uploads, sparse data, areas it hasn't been taught. Honesty builds trust faster than silence.
"SID can't yet see payment status — it knows when invoices were sent, not when they were paid."
💬
Every email invites feedback
Each email closes with a reply prompt. Zach and Jacob's replies are how SID learns what language, what detail, and what ordering actually works for them. Replies log to sid_report_feedback.
"Reply to shape future updates — more detail, less, or a different order? Tell SID."
🎯
Audience-aware routing
Zach and Jacob can receive the same or different versions of any cadence. As preferences emerge — one prefers ops, one prefers commercial — the system adapts per recipient per cadence without separate edge function invocations.
Config stored per recipient in sid_report_schedule.
