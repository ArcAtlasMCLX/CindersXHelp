Revenue Target Planner

Set annual revenue targets by P&L specialism, apply seasonal weightings, and lock plans for comparison against actuals.

The six P&L specialisms

Every job type in CindersX maps to one of six revenue specialisms. These are the lines on your P&L — not job-level categories, but the commercial service lines that your revenue target is built from.

Extract Cleans Fire Damper Testing Fire Door Works Air Ventilation Cleans Fire Stopping Works Fire Damper Remedials

All 17 active job types are already mapped. You can review and adjust mappings at Job Settings → Specialisms, and you can reassign any job type at Job Settings → Job Types → General.

Creating a plan

1

Open Targets and set the headline number

Go to Management → Targets. Enter the year (e.g. 2027), your overall ex-VAT revenue target, and whether the financial year starts in January or July. Click Generate Plan.

2

Review the system-calculated grid

CindersX distributes the target across the six specialisms and twelve months using historical blend weightings. The grid shows months as rows and specialisms as columns — no horizontal scrolling. Every cell is rounded to the nearest £1,000.

Reading the % row: Directly below the column headers sits an editable percentage for each specialism. These show each specialism's share of the annual total and always sum to 100%.
3

Adjust at the specialism or cell level

Two ways to edit:

Specialism %

Change a specialism's share — e.g. raise Fire Door Works from 26% to 30%. All other specialism percentages scale down proportionally. Monthly amounts update instantly across all 12 rows.

Individual cell

Click any cell and type a value. The cell snaps to the nearest £1k on blur and is highlighted in amber to indicate it has been manually overridden.

Press Tab between percentage inputs or Enter to commit. Unsaved changes are held in memory until you click Save Changes.
4

Save and lock

Click Save Changes at any point to persist edits. When the plan is finalised, click Lock Plan. Locked plans are read-only and can only be unlocked by a super admin. The system will warn you if the plan total doesn't match your headline target before you lock.

One plan per period: CindersX prevents you creating two plans for the same year and period start. The form will warn you, and the database enforces it as a hard constraint.

Quick reference

Reset to System

Discards all manual overrides and restores every cell to the system-calculated value based on your weightings.

Lock Plan

Makes the plan read-only. Locked plans can be read by all internal users and used as the target baseline in reporting.

Unlock Plan

Super admins only. Reverts a locked plan to draft so it can be edited. Use with care — any reports using this plan as baseline will reflect the change.

Delete Plan

Available on draft plans only (from the list or the plan header). Permanently removes the plan and all 72 line entries. Locked plans must be unlocked first.

What this enables

Actual vs target, per specialism per month

Once invoiced revenue is tagged to job types — which are now all mapped to specialisms — CindersX can produce a live actual vs target view. You'll see which service lines are running ahead or behind, not just the company total.

Dashboard target bars

Locked plans feed the Management Dashboard. The SID intelligence layer can reference the locked plan to contextualise revenue commentary — e.g. "Fire Door Works is £42k ahead of the April target."

Year-on-year comparison

Because plans are versioned by year, you can compare 2026 actuals against 2026 targets, then use those actuals to inform how the 2027 weightings are set — closing the planning loop.

Mid-year reforecast

The July period start option is there for a reason. At mid-year you can create a second plan (Jan–Dec, same year, different period start) as a reforecast — your H1 actuals inform the H2 numbers — and lock it as the revised baseline.
